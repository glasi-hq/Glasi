Definitions

Glasi Ltd
The company providing the Glasi platform and services. Registered in England and Wales, company number 17147716.
Platform

Subscriber
A bar, venue or hospitality business that has entered into a subscription agreement with Glasi Ltd and has an active subscription.
Glasi Worker

Shift Request

The fee payable by the Subscriber to Glasi Ltd for each completed shift, calculated at the agreed hourly rate multiplied by the confirmed shift length.
Section 2

About Glasi

Glasi Ltd operates a technology platform that connects bars and hospitality venues with pre-vetted local workers available for short-notice glass collection and table clearing shifts. Glasi Ltd acts as an intermediary platform provider and does not itself employ the workers deployed to subscriber venues.

Workers are engaged and paid via a third-party payroll provider. Glasi Ltd manages the recruitment, vetting, scheduling and dispatch of workers on behalf of subscribers.

Section 5

Requesting a Glasi

Shift Requests must be submitted via the Glasi bar manager portal at glasi.co.uk/manager.html. Requests submitted by other means (including by telephone or messaging) are accepted at Glasi Ltd's sole discretion and do not carry the same binding confirmation as a platform submission.

To submit a Shift Request, the authorised bar manager must:

  • Be logged in with their authorised account credentials
  • Select the required shift length (2, 3 or 4 hours)
  • Confirm agreement to these Terms of Service by ticking the confirmation checkbox
  • Confirm the request using their personal 4-digit PIN

Submission of a Shift Request via the platform constitutes a formal, binding request and agreement to pay the applicable Shift Fee if the request is fulfilled within the Fulfilment Window.

Shift Requests may only be submitted during an active Session in the Subscriber's zone. Glasi Ltd schedules Sessions in advance based on anticipated demand. The Subscriber should contact Glasi Ltd in advance to discuss session scheduling for their venue.

Section 6

Binding Nature of Requests

Once a Shift Request has been submitted, it is binding and cannot be cancelled or withdrawn by the Subscriber.

If a Glasi Worker is dispatched to the venue within the Fulfilment Window, the Subscriber is obligated to accept the worker and pay the full Shift Fee for the agreed shift length, regardless of whether the worker is ultimately needed for the full duration.

If the Subscriber refuses to accept a dispatched worker upon arrival without reasonable cause, Glasi Ltd reserves the right to issue an invoice for the full Shift Fee and to take action to recover payment.

Repeated refusal to accept dispatched workers may result in suspension or termination of the Subscriber's account.

Section 7

The 30-Minute Fulfilment Window

Upon submission of a Shift Request, Glasi Ltd will make reasonable efforts to dispatch an available and suitable Glasi Worker to the Subscriber's venue within 30 minutes of the request being submitted.

The Fulfilment Window begins at the time the Shift Request is submitted on the platform. If a Glasi Worker is dispatched within this 30-minute window, the request is considered fulfilled and the Shift Fee becomes payable.

If Glasi Ltd is unable to dispatch a Glasi Worker within the 30-minute Fulfilment Window, the Shift Request expires automatically. No Shift Fee is charged for an expired, unfulfilled request.

The Fulfilment Window cannot be extended or paused by either party.

Glasi Ltd does not guarantee that a Glasi Worker will be available to fulfil any given Shift Request. A subscription gives the Subscriber the right to submit Shift Requests -- it does not guarantee that those requests will be fulfilled.

The availability of Glasi Workers depends on a number of factors including the number of workers registered in the Subscriber's zone, the time and day of the request, competing requests from other Subscribers, and worker availability at the time of the request.

Glasi Ltd will make all reasonable commercial efforts to fulfil Shift Requests submitted during active Sessions. However, the Subscriber acknowledges that fulfilment cannot be guaranteed and that an unfulfilled request does not constitute a breach of these Terms by Glasi Ltd.

No refund or credit of the subscription fee will be issued solely on the basis of an unfulfilled Shift Request.

Section 9

Shift Fees and Invoicing

Where a Shift Request is fulfilled, Glasi Ltd will invoice the Subscriber for the applicable Shift Fee. The Shift Fee is calculated at £30.00 per hour multiplied by the shift length confirmed at the time of the request.

Shift fees are subject to VAT at the standard rate of 20% once Glasi Ltd is VAT registered. Invoices issued prior to VAT registration will be reissued to include VAT once the VAT registration number is confirmed. The Subscriber agrees to pay the VAT-inclusive amount upon reissue.

Invoices will be issued following the completion of each shift and will include the shift reference number (GS reference), the worker name, venue, date, clock-in and clock-out times, shift length, hourly rate and total amount due.

Shift Fees are separate from and in addition to the monthly subscription fee.
